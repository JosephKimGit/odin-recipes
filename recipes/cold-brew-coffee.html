Cold Brew Coffee

Description

A perfect cup of cold brew coffee is the best way to kickstart your morning - especially for those summer days.

Cold brew coffee is generally sweeter and less acidic than other methods of brewing making it tastier but more time-consuming. This recipe guide will focus on a french press method of brewing but there are several variations you can use such as a mason jar + paper filter combination.

Follow this simple recipe to enjoy a colder cup of joe.

Ingredients

  • French Press (aka Coffee press)
  • Grounded Coffee beans
  • Milk/Cream and Sugar/Sweetener
  • Filtered Water
  • Ice cubes

Instructions

  1. Coarsely ground your coffee beans of choice. The ratio of coffee to water is variable to preference, but a rule of thumb is a ratio of 1:8 of coffee to water in grams.
  2. Add the grounded coffee into your french press and add your filtered water
  3. Mix around the water and grounded coffee to ensure all the coffee beans get wet
  4. Put the french press into your refrigerator and keep it there for 12-24 hours or longer
  5. When enough time has passed, press down on your french press and pour out a cup of coffee! Add ice, cream, sugar as desired.
